💡 What is LCM?

The LCM (Least Common Multiple) of two numbers is the smallest number that is divisible by both numbers.

Example: LCM of 4 and 6 is 12 — because 12 is the smallest number divisible by both 4 and 6.

Tip: Try listing multiples or use the GCD trick (if you remember it 😄)!
